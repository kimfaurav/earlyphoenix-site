Practice Policies

We fully comply with CQC regulations and have policies and protocols in place for your safety. All policies are available at reception.

The practice aims to meet the requirements of the Data Protection Act 2018, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the guidelines on the Information Commissioner's website as well as our professional guidelines and requirements.

The data controller and information governance lead is Rakhi Kakad. You will be asked to provide personal information when joining the practice for the purpose of providing optimum health care.

Your personal data rights include:

  • The right to be informed
  • The right of access
  • The right to rectification
  • The right to erasure (clinical records must be retained for a certain time period)
  • The right to restrict processing
  • The right to data portability
  • The right to object

Retention period for special data in patient records is a minimum of 10 years. Staff records: 6 years. Other personal data: 2 years after last processed. ICO Number: ZA241512.

We welcome comments, suggestions and complaints so that we can continually improve our service to you.

Please contact the practice in person, by phone, letter or email if you have a comment, suggestion or complaint. We take complaints very seriously and have an effective procedure to resolve any problems in the shortest possible time.

You can read more about our procedures in our complaints policy, available from reception. Should you wish to write to us: 9a Hill Avenue, Amersham, Bucks, HP6 5BD.

The practice is committed to complying with the requirements of legislation governing patient confidentiality including: Access to Health Records 1990, Caldicott Guidelines 1997, Confidentiality Code of Practice 1998, Data Protection Act 2018, GDPR and the current GDC Standards.

No personal information given or received in confidence is passed on to anyone else without the patient's consent. Patient information is kept confidential even after death.

Information may only be released without consent in exceptional circumstances such as: if it is in the best interests of the public, if a patient may be at risk of significant harm, or if required by a court order.

Our practice has a safety culture where patient safety is at the forefront of everyone's minds. All team members are trained in safeguarding of children and vulnerable adults.

We follow the latest infection prevention guidelines including those from NHS England, use single-use or sterilised instruments, decontaminate work areas between patients, and maintain high standards of personal hygiene.

Dental unit waterlines are disinfected and kept clear. Waste is handled according to current regulations. We always welcome patients' questions about patient safety at our practice.

Due to the Data Protection Policy, the practice requires written permission to send text/email reminders or recalls. Your personal information is never passed to third parties for their own marketing, sales or promotions.

You can withdraw your consent at any time. Please note that reminders are provided as a courtesy and it is the patient's responsibility to remember their own appointment.

Accessibility Notice: Please note that our surgeries are located up a steep flight of stairs and may not be suitable for patients with mobility difficulties. For health and safety reasons, our staff are not trained to assist with access and are unable to provide physical support. We apologise for any inconvenience.
