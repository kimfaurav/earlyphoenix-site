Key Findings & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Market size: 8,354 active listings across 5 platforms, dominated by Ball Pythons (38%) — median price £150
  • Regulatory gaps: 57% of the market involves CITES-listed species, yet 59% of Hermann's Tortoise listings have no Article 10 certificate reference
  • Illegal species: 1 Yellow-Bellied Slider listing found on Pets4Homes — a species banned from sale since August 2016 under Invasive Alien Species Regulations
  • DWA loophole: 0 species require a Dangerous Wild Animals licence, despite Burmese pythons (5m), boa constrictors (4m) and venomous species being freely traded
  • Breeder-dominated: 90.1% breeders (incl. pet stores), 9.8% private sellers, 0.1% rescues
  • No structured welfare data: Unlike cats & dogs, reptile platforms provide zero welfare checkboxes — only 8.3% mention any welfare keyword

Recommendations

  • Flag banned species: Platforms should block or flag listings for species banned under the Invasive Alien Species Regulation (e.g. Yellow-Bellied Slider, Red-Eared Slider)
  • Enforce Article 10: Require sellers of Annex A species (Hermann's, Spur-Thighed Tortoise) to upload or reference their APHA certificate before listing goes live
  • Structured welfare fields: Engage platforms to introduce captive bred status, CITES documentation, and health checks as structured fields — matching cat & dog standards
  • DWA review: Large constrictor species (Burmese Python, Boa Constrictor) are freely sold without any licence — the DWA Act schedule has not been updated since 2007
  • Longitudinal tracking: Establish monthly scraping cadence across all 5 platforms to monitor compliance trends and market shifts
