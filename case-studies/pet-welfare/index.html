UK Pet Marketplace Welfare Intelligence

An automated system that monitors pet sales across the UK's online marketplaces — giving welfare organisations, policymakers, and enforcement teams the data they've never had.

The Problem

Around a million puppies are sold in the UK each year. Most are listed on online marketplaces — but nobody was systematically tracking what was being sold, by whom, or whether sellers were meeting basic welfare and licensing requirements.

Welfare organisations relied on manual spot-checks. Enforcement teams didn't know where to focus. Policymakers lacked the evidence to push for change.

What We Built

An automated intelligence pipeline that collects, deduplicates, and analyses pet sale listings across 9 of the UK's major online marketplaces — covering dogs, cats, reptiles, amphibians, and small furries.

The system captures listing data, seller profiles, pricing, welfare declarations, breeding license status, breed information, and geographic location — then structures it into actionable intelligence.

Key Findings

These marketplaces capture approximately 75% of first-time puppy sales in the UK — making this the most comprehensive view of the market available.

  • Welfare compliance varies wildly by platform. Some collect 6 of 7 welfare indicators from sellers. Others collect none.
  • Breeding license gaps. One major platform with ~38% market share doesn't collect breeding license data at all — a significant regulatory blind spot.
  • Geographic hotspots. High-volume listing concentrations in specific towns point to potential unlicensed commercial breeding operations.
  • Breed welfare concerns. ~10% of dog listings are brachycephalic breeds with documented health issues. Platform breed bans (where enforced) are effective.
  • Stale listings. Some platforms have listings over 6 months old still active — misleading buyers and inflating market size.

What This Enables

  • Enforcement targeting — identify unlicensed high-volume sellers and geographic hotspots for local authority investigation
  • Platform accountability — compare welfare standards across marketplaces and push for minimum data requirements
  • Policy evidence — data-backed arguments for regulatory change, including extending breeding regulations to cats
  • Trend detection — monthly tracking reveals shifts in pricing, breed popularity, and compliance over time

How It Works

The system runs automatically with no manual intervention. Automated collectors extract structured data from each marketplace, deduplicate across sources, and feed into analysis pipelines. Results are delivered as interactive dashboards and data decks.
