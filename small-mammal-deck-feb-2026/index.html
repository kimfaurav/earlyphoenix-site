Key Findings & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Market size: 4,678 active listings across 5 platforms, with 11 species and 124 breeds identified
  • Rabbit-dominated market: Rabbits account for 58.3% of all listings. Mini Lop is the most-listed breed (721 listings), followed by Lionhead and Netherland Dwarf
  • Welfare gap: 26.3% of listings involve welfare-flagged breeds (lop-eared 21.4%, Netherland Dwarf, Sugar Gliders, African Pygmy Hedgehogs). Structured welfare fields are rabbit-only (18.4% vs 0.3%) and 3 of 5 platforms collect none
  • Sellers: 2,288 unique sellers; 53.4% self-declare as Breeders on P4H (only platform with seller type). Just 11 cross-platform. 1,630 locations mapped — broadly distributed, no hotspots

Recommendations

  • Neuter status field: Only 2 of 4,678 listings are marked as neutered (0.04%), yet 7% mention it in descriptions. Platforms should add a mandatory neuter field and encourage neutering — especially for rabbits
  • Welfare transparency: Expand structured welfare fields to all small furries — only 15.2% average coverage, non-rabbit species invisible, and 3 of 5 platforms collect nothing. Monitor welfare-flagged breeds (26.3% of market)
  • Licensing compliance: Only 29 listings (0.6%) mention licences — cross-reference high-volume sellers and geographic data with local authority licensing records
  • Longitudinal tracking: Establish monthly data collection across all 5 platforms to track pricing shifts, breed popularity trends, and welfare improvements over time
Data collected February 2026. 4,678 listings analysed across 5 platforms.
