Key Findings & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Market size: 3,231 active listings across 5 platforms, with 11 species and 135 breeds identified
  • Rabbit-dominated market: Rabbits account for 66.3% of all listings. Mini Lop is the most-listed breed (600 listings), followed by Lionhead and Netherland Dwarf
  • Welfare gap: 27.3% of listings involve welfare-flagged breeds (lop-eared 22.4%, Netherland Dwarf, Sugar Gliders, African Pygmy Hedgehogs). Structured welfare fields are rabbit-only (17.1% vs 0.4%) and 3 of 5 platforms collect none
  • Sellers: 1,931 unique sellers; 54.4% self-declare as Breeders on P4H (only platform with seller type). Just 5 cross-platform. 1,226 locations mapped — broadly distributed, no hotspots

Recommendations

  • Welfare transparency: Encourage platforms to add structured welfare fields for all small furries — currently only 20.4% average coverage and non-rabbit species are invisible
  • Welfare-flagged breed monitoring: Track lop-eared breeds, Netherland Dwarfs, Sugar Gliders and African Pygmy Hedgehogs — 27.3% of the market warrants ongoing welfare scrutiny
  • Licensing compliance: Only 29 listings (0.9%) mention licences — cross-reference high-volume sellers and geographic data with local authority licensing records
  • Longitudinal tracking: Establish monthly scraping cadence across all 5 platforms to track pricing shifts, breed popularity trends, and welfare improvements over time
Data collected February 2026. 3,231 listings analysed across 5 platforms.
