Key Findings & Recommendations

Key Findings

  • Market growing: 5,155 active listings in April — up +13.9% from 4,524 in March and recovering from a February peak of 4,678. Median price rose from £45.00 to £50.00 (++11.1%); the share of listings priced £50–£100 grew +1.9pp as budget-end stock thinned
  • Rabbit-dominated market: Rabbits & breeds account for ~62% of all listings. Mini Lop is the most-listed breed (863 listings). Continental Giant entered the top 10 this month; Chinchilla dropped out. 5 platforms cover 11 species and 135 breeds
  • Welfare gap: 25.9% of listings involve welfare-flagged breeds (lop-eared 19.7%, Netherland Dwarf, Sugar Gliders, African Pygmy Hedgehogs). Structured welfare fields cover only P4H and Petify — 3 of 5 platforms collect nothing. Vaccination rate stable at ~13.8% among those platforms that ask
  • Persistent high-volume sellers: 3,269 unique sellers in April; 9 of the top 15 appear across all 3 months. Top cumulative seller: jaydenlane12 (31 listings across Feb–Apr, Ripley). Only 26 listings (0.5%) carry a licence number — all on P4H

Recommendations

  • Mandate neuter status: Only 0 listings are marked neutered (0.0%), yet 6.8% reference it in descriptions. Platforms should add this as a structured field — especially critical for rabbits, where unneutered pairs are routinely sold together
  • Extend welfare fields beyond rabbits: Average welfare coverage is 14.9%. Guinea pigs, hamsters, rats and chinchillas — collectively 19.1%+ of the market — have zero structured welfare capture on most platforms. Welfare-flagged breeds (25.9% of listings) warrant priority attention
  • Flag persistent high-volume FreeAds sellers: FreeAds grew +27.0% this month and hosts the majority of top cumulative sellers — none with platform-visible licence data. Cross-reference names, locations, and listing frequency against local authority licensing records
  • Continue monthly data collection: Three months of data already show meaningful trends — price uplift, species shifts, seller persistence. Expanding to 6-month and 12-month windows will enable seasonal baselines and early detection of welfare-concerning volume spikes
Data collected April 2026. 5,155 listings analysed across 5 platforms. Trends compared against March 2026 (4,524) and February 2026 (4,678) snapshots.
